I am a recent graduate of Reed College, where I earned my B.A. in Physics. After graduation, I was hired by the college to continue researching the subject of my senior thesis, a novel experimental approach to measuring the mass of cosmic-ray muons.

My work focused on developing Monte-Carlo simulations of coupled electron-photon showers in scintillation detectors to predict the characteristic energy deposition of the muon's decay products.

More generally, my interests in physics include astronomy, optics, particle physics, and nonlinear dynamics. I am most interested in work related to scientific computation, statistical modeling, and programming simulations.
